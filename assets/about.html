Mother and Child health care Association is a Cameroon based charity organization, dedicated to standing up for families especially within the reproductive age, and children under the age of five years. With live experience, we advocate for the rights of every pregnant woman and children for proper health care and the right to live. We are particularly implicated in the prevention of mother to child transmission of HIV (PMCT), the prevention, and reduction of malnutrition in pregnant women and children under 5 years.

We raise and grant funds and deliver a wide range of high-impact programs and services in partnership with health institutions around the country. We are mostly work with those in the suburbs where public health has no influence or presence and the people have no access to health care. We provide assistance to pregnant women and children living in remote areas of the country; Deprived, Excluded and Vulnerable to health hazards that would otherwise have been prevented.

MOCHCA also works together with organizations giving psychological relieve to war victims, those women and children who have experienced rape, and all sexual violence especially those who get pregnant by this process, we provide support for their health care and help them overcome the trauma, break the cycle and gain strength and resilience to survive.

Assists vulnerable
women
Founded by Madam Bezejuh Emmerencia in 2017. MOCHCA’s primary vision is to reduce maternal, Neonatal and child mortality as much as possible through provision of proper assistance for appropriate health care. Pregnant woman and children below the age of 5 who constitute the vulnerable groups in the underdeveloped society particularly have many times been subject to become victims of circumstances leading to their death either because they are poor and so cannot access good health care, are malnourished, or are ignorant of the importance of preventive health measures. Our goal is to ensure that every woman of child bearing age, particularly the pregnant women and mothers of children under five years understand the importance and be able to access proper antenatal care, child health care services, are well fed to prevent malnutrition as well as vaccinated against all preventable diseases and also prevented
